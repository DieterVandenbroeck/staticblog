Would you kill a bunch of school kids or an elderly couple?

Dieter Vandenbroeck | 24 Nov 2015
"

Self-driving cars are a hot topic: they're safer, can reduce traffic jams, reduce expensive cab fares, and are just really really cool. But maybe not yet for tomorrow: Tesla's new update makes the Model S a self-driving car (although they warn you to still keep your hands on the wheel), but many videos appearing the days after make it clear that the autopilot software needs some tweaking (it seems to hate the car and the people within). Google is still heavily testing on dedicated test tracks and open streets (with a driver present and in control at all times), but needs some additional time to handle the last bits of unpredictable situations. And then there's other car manufacturers and tech companies that are also pursuing this dream.

Even more prevailing are the discussions on some ethical dilemmas. Ultimately, almost all questions are in one of the following two categories: is it better to sacrifice the life of the driver to prevent more deaths, and is it better to kill a few in an accident than many?

As an example of the first, consider a situation in which you're driving with 3 friends in your self-driving car, and in order to prevent a lethal accident (in which all 4 of you will die) the car can move to the sidewalk and kill 1 pedestrian (but probably save all 4 in the car).

Another example, aligned with the other category, is the following: you're in a self-driving car, and you'll either crash into a group of school kids or (when taking evasive action) crash into an elderly couple.

Can a car correctly comprehend such a situation and make calculated decisions? Should the driver be allowed via a button to preregister his preference (e.g. save me)? Should the amount of people involved always be the most important factor?

Difficult questions without a clear answer. In my opinion, there of course is no single answer, but a very lengthy and difficult discussion to discuss all options. To lighten the mood a little bit, the guys from SMBC comics propose an economical approach: https://smbc-comics.com/index.php?id=3831
