What your electricity bill is telling about you

Dieter Vandenbroeck | 25 Feb 2014
"

About 2 weeks ago, I attended the Owasp Belgium chapter meeting where George Danezis presented his work on smart metering privacy. This presentation included so many surprises and really cool solutions that I will share the first part here.

These days, a common saying regarding privacy and collecting certain data is “if you've got nothing to hide, you’ve got nothing to fear”. While not going into this regarding how wrong this is, it neglects the fact that it’s virtually impossible for everyone to understand the impact that (the knowledge of) certain data can have. This is also the case when the data involved is “electricity usage data”.

The best known example of using electricity usage data is the detection of cannabis farms: because the installation required for a decent cannabis farm uses a lot of electricity, the electricity usage of individual houses can be checked against the average. The one with the unusually high electricity usage (and the unusual heat-image) probably is the sought farm.

The coolest example is from Germany in the 1970s. Law enforcement figured out that houses that had their rent paid cash (and in time), with a low energy consumption –except for a couple of days a year- were the safe houses of Rolf Heissler, a member of the Red Army Faction. Small detail: this practice was ruled illegal at the time.

Finally, it’s also possible to figure out what websites you are visiting or what TV shows you watch, what your sleeping pattern is, or in the end, just exactly what you are doing in your own home. Yeah, it’s rather difficult for most people to grasp what information can be deducted from your electricity usage information. If this information would be shared with for example insurance companies, it will become hard to deny that unhealthy lifestyle.

If you’re interested in the solution –involving smart meters and zero-knowledge protocols-, make sure to check out George Danezis’ work!
